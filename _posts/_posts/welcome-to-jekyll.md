The G-RAM_G Act, 2025, raises serious concerns for agricultural labour. A key question is why there is a need to pause NREGA work during sowing and harvesting periods. Such a provision appears to favour large farmers while increasing the vulnerability of agricultural labourers.

There are many constraints that agriculature labour are not paid minimum wage specialy women, by the large farmers. 
NREGA has directly increased the bargaining power of agricultural labour and strengthened the financial independence of rural women by providing an alternative source of employment at minimum wages.

For instance, during the cotton harvesting season, large farmers have a significantly higher demand for labour, but often offer very low wages. In the absence of NREGA, agricultural labourers are compelled to accept whatever wage is offered due to a lack of alternatives. 
However, when NREGA work is available, labourers can choose to work to earn at least the minimum wage.

Therefore, the new Act will become a mechanism for exploiting agricultural labour.
